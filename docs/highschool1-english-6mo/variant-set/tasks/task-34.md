## Passage Text (exact)
```
A particularly powerful way in which human beings adapt and adjust to their circumstances is by _______________ that they yield. For instance, a child who gets praised by her teacher for doing an exercise correctly is likely to continue doing the exercise in that way. A dancer who feels that a particular conditioning exercise is not giving any results is likely to give that exercise up in favor of another one that seems more promising. In general, we tend to do things that bring us some kind of benefit or act in ways that help us avoid some kind of problem, discomfort, or disadvantage. We also tend to do fewer things (or stop doing things) that lead to problems, discomfort, or disadvantages, and we tend to avoid acting in ways that do not bring any benefit or reward.
```
Original blank: "by ___ that they yield" with answer = ① "changing behaviors in light of the consequences."

## Questions

### Q1: 빈칸추론 (2점) — NEW blank
- **발문**: `다음 빈칸에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Blank**: "we tend to do things that bring us some kind of _____"
- **Correct**: ③ advantage
- **Choices**:
  - ① difficulty
  - ② uncertainty
  - ③ advantage  ← CORRECT (= "benefit" in the passage; we do things that help us, i.e., give advantage)
  - ④ obligation
  - ⑤ distraction
- **Why hard**: Must understand the whole conditional logic: we act to gain something good. "obligation" is tempting (some do things because they have to). The context is explicitly about *choosing* beneficial behaviors.

### Q2: 영어 주제 (2점)
- **발문**: `윗글의 주제로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Correct**: ①
- **Choices**:
  - ① how the consequences of behavior shape what people choose to do
  - ② why children need external praise to develop good habits
  - ③ the difference between motivation and discipline in human behavior
  - ④ how discomfort can sometimes lead to greater personal achievement
  - ⑤ the role of repetition in building effective exercise routines
- **Why hard**: ② narrows it to children (passage uses children as ONE example). ⑤ focuses on exercise (one example only). ④ directly contradicts the passage (we AVOID discomfort, not grow from it).
