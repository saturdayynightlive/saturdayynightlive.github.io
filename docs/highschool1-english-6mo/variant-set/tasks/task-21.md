# Task: Passage 21 — Shop for Words (함의추론) [3점]

## Passage Text (exact)
```
A classical ideal of truth and knowledge is the concept of a market for ideas: as thinking individuals, we encounter competing ideas, we evaluate them for their best fit to reality, and then let the best and brightest facts form the basis of our beliefs. That would be the scientist's way, in theory at least. But in practice, most of the time we are in a market for justifications. We shop for words not because they contain ideas, but because they contain stories about ideas. Often we are not seeking statements as facts to help us figure out what we should believe. Usually we already know what we believe. What we seek are statements to justify those beliefs — the lawyer's way. Language might be good for this function but we can use it to do better. By treating language with greater respect, we can achieve more than simple self-defense.
```

## Passage Modifications
1. **Blank** (Q1): Replace "justifications" with `\rule{3.5cm}{0.4pt}` → "most of the time we are in a market for _____"
2. **네모 ×3** (Q2): Apply to three locations:
   - (A): "we evaluate them for their best \nemo{fit / fitting} to reality"
   - (B): "let the best and brightest facts \nemo{form / to form} the basis"
   - (C): "Language might be good for this function but we can use it to do \nemo{good / better}"
3. **Underline** (Q3 서술형): none needed

## Questions

### Q1: 빈칸추론 (3점)
- **발문**: `다음 빈칸에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은? [3점]`
- **Blank**: "most of the time we are in a market for _____"
- **Correct**: ③ rationalizations
- **Choices**:
  - ① inspirations
  - ② innovations
  - ③ rationalizations  ← CORRECT (= post-hoc justifications for existing beliefs)
  - ④ contradictions
  - ⑤ observations
- **Why hard**: "justifications" was the original word; "rationalizations" is a harder synonym. "observations" tempting as it's science-like. "inspirations" attracts those who misread the tone.

### Q2: 어법 네모 (2점)
- **발문**: `(A), (B), (C)의 각 네모 안에서 어법에 맞는 표현으로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- (A) "fit" (noun after "their best") → fit ✓ / fitting ✗
- (B) "form" (bare infinitive after causative "let") → form ✓ / to form ✗
- (C) "better" (comparative, "can do better") → better ✓ / good ✗
- **Correct**: ② fit / form / better
- **Full choice table**:
  ```
       (A)       (B)        (C)
  ①  fitting   form      better
  ②  fit       form      better   ← CORRECT
  ③  fit       to form   better
  ④  fitting   to form   good
  ⑤  fit       form      good
  ```
