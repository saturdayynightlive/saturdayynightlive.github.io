# Task: Passage 38 — 제스처와 기억 (문장 삽입)

## Passage Text (exact, with ①~⑤ insertion markers)
```
If you gesture while describing an event, do you think you'll be able to recall that event better than if you don't gesture? ( ① ) We had adults watch videos of toy objects, animals, and people performing various, sometimes odd, actions: a chicken sliding to a policeman, a jogger bending down to touch his toes, a fence swinging shut on its own. ( ② ) We then tested the adults' memories of these events immediately after the descriptions and three weeks later. ( ③ ) To see if we'd get the same effects if people gestured on their own, we did the study again, but this time we gave the adults no instructions about gesture. ( ④ ) We found the same patterns: people remembered items on which they had gestured spontaneously better than items on which they had not gestured. ( ⑤ ) Producing gesture along with speech makes the information encoded in that speech memorable.
* encode: 부호화하다
```

Original insertion sentence (in box): "Adults who were told to gesture while describing the events remembered more than adults who were told not to gesture, particularly several weeks later."
Original answer: ③

## Passage Modifications
Use a DIFFERENT insertion sentence and DIFFERENT correct answer position.

**New insertion sentence (in box)**:
"This suggests that the act of gesturing itself, not just following instructions to gesture, plays a role in strengthening memory."

**Correct answer**: ④ (after the second experiment results, before the final summary)
- Rationale: After ④ reports finding the same patterns spontaneously, the new sentence draws the conclusion that gesturing ITSELF (not just following instructions) helps memory. This leads naturally into ⑤'s general claim.

## Questions

### Q1: 문장 삽입 (2점)
- **발문**: `글의 흐름으로 보아, 주어진 문장이 들어가기에 가장 적절한 곳을 고르시오.`
- **Insertion sentence**: "This suggests that the act of gesturing itself, not just following instructions to gesture, plays a role in strengthening memory."
- **Correct**: ④
- **Why hard**: ③ seems possible (before we describe the second experiment). But ③ is where the second experiment is introduced, not where its results are given. The sentence draws a CONCLUSION from results, so it must come AFTER the results (④).

### Q2: 빈칸추론 (2점)
Add a blank: Replace "spontaneously" with `\rule{3.5cm}{0.4pt}` in ④:
"people remembered items on which they had gestured _____ better than items on which they had not gestured."
- **발문**: `다음 빈칸에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Correct**: ② of their own accord
- **Choices**:
  - ① in response to direct instruction
  - ② of their own accord  ← CORRECT (= spontaneously = without being told to)
  - ③ at a rapid pace
  - ④ in a repetitive manner
  - ⑤ with great exaggeration
- **Why hard**: ① is the OPPOSITE of spontaneously (this was the second experiment, where no instructions were given). Students may mix up which experiment is being described.

### Q3: 서술형 — 요지 한글 단답 (2점)
- **발문**: `[서술형] 이 글의 요지를 한 문장(40자 이내)으로 우리말로 쓰시오.`

- **Grading**: "제스처 + 기억 향상" 연결이 있으면 정답
