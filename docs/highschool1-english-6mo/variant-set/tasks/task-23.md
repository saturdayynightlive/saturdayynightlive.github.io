# Task: Passage 23 — Guilt vs Shame (주제)

## Passage Text (exact)
```
Shame is about who we are, and guilt is about our behaviors. We feel guilty when we hold up something we've done or failed to do against the kind of person we want to be. It's an uncomfortable feeling, but one that's helpful. When we apologize for something we've done, make amends to others, or change a behavior that we don't feel good about, guilt is most often the motivator. Guilt is just as powerful as shame, but its effect is often positive while shame often is destructive. When we see people apologize, make amends, or replace negative behaviors with more positive ones, guilt is often the motivator, not shame. In fact, in my research, I found that shame corrodes the part of us that believes we can change and do better.
* amends: 보상, 배상  ** corrode: 좀먹다, 약화시키다
```

## Passage Modifications
1. **Blank** (Q1): Replace "destructive" with `\rule{3cm}{0.4pt}` → "its effect is often positive while shame often is _____"
2. No other structural changes.

## Questions

### Q1: 빈칸추론 (2점)
- **발문**: `다음 빈칸에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Blank**: "shame often is _____"
- **Correct**: ③ corrosive
- **Choices**:
  - ① motivating
  - ② constructive
  - ③ corrosive  ← CORRECT (= destructive; the passage's verb "corrodes" reinforces this)
  - ④ unconscious
  - ⑤ inevitable
- **Why hard**: ① "motivating" is the word used for guilt (not shame) — attractive trap. ② "constructive" is the antonym of the correct idea. Must know "corrosive" = harmful/eroding.

### Q2: 영어 제목 (2점)
- **발문**: `윗글의 제목으로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Correct**: ③
- **Choices**:
  - ① Shame: The Stronger Motivator for Changing Behavior
  - ② How Guilt and Shame Work Together to Build Character
  - ③ Guilt Drives Positive Change; Shame Tears Us Down
  - ④ The Role of Apology in Overcoming Guilt and Shame
  - ⑤ Why We Should Embrace Both Guilt and Shame Equally
- **Why hard**: ① reverses the message (shame is NOT the stronger motivator). ② implies they work together — passage contrasts them. ⑤ says "equally" — passage clearly sides with guilt over shame.

### Q3: 요약문 (A)(B) (2점)
- **발문**: `다음은 윗글을 요약한 것이다. 빈칸 (A), (B)에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Summary text**: *Unlike shame, which (A)_____ our belief in self-improvement, guilt tends to (B)_____ positive behavioral change by pushing us to apologize and make amends.*
- **Correct**: (A) undermines / (B) drive (= ③)
- **Choice table**:
  ```
       (A)              (B)
  ①  reinforces        suppress
  ②  reinforces        encourage
  ③  undermines        drive         ← CORRECT
  ④  undermines        prevent
  ⑤  challenges        delay
  ```
- **Why hard**: (A) "reinforces" is the opposite of "corrodes" — very attractive trap. (B) "prevent" makes (A) correct but (B) contradicts passage's claim about guilt.
