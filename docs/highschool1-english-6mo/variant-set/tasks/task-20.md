# Task: Passage 20 — 자녀 가치 교육 (주장)

## Passage Text (exact)
```
Remember how you learned to drive or cook? You practiced while someone coached you, reminding you what to do until you were able to coach yourself and then, eventually, do it automatically. Children learn values much the same way. They practice different kinds of behavior, while, you, as coach, help focus their attention on what is important and on fine-tuning important skills. You support them with your praise, encouragement and gentle reminders. If you don't coach your child, she will find her coaches elsewhere and be guided by the values of the media, her peers and anyone else who captures her interest. So, step up to the plate, don't be afraid and help your child learn how to be a good person, step by step.
```

## Passage Modifications
1. **Blank** (Q1): Replace "fine-tuning" with `\rule{3cm}{0.4pt}` in the sentence "help focus their attention on what is important and on _____ important skills."
2. No other modifications — passage otherwise original.

## Questions

### Q1: 빈칸추론 (2점)
- **발문**: `다음 빈칸에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Blank**: "on _____ important skills"
- **Correct**: ② refining
- **Choices**:
  - ① memorizing
  - ② refining  ← CORRECT (synonym of fine-tuning: polishing/perfecting)
  - ③ ignoring
  - ④ replacing
  - ⑤ evaluating
- **Why hard**: "evaluating" is attractive but the passage is about *improving*, not assessing. "memorizing" is tempting from "practiced." Must understand coaching = refinement.

### Q2: 영어 제목 (2점)
- **발문**: `윗글의 제목으로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Correct**: ④
- **Choices**:
  - ① Why Children Learn Better from Peers Than from Parents
  - ② The Role of Media in Shaping Children's Behavior
  - ③ How Automatic Skills Develop Through Repetition
  - ④ Coach Your Child: Be the Guide They Need to Grow Well
  - ⑤ Encouraging Independence: When to Stop Coaching Your Child
- **Why hard**: ① is tempting (passage mentions peers as alternative coaches — negatively). ⑤ flips the message (passage says DON'T stop coaching). ④ captures the imperative "step up to the plate."
