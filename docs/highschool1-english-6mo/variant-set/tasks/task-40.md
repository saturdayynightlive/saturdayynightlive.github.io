# Task: Passage 40 — 무의식적 위험 감지 (요약)

## Passage Text (exact)
```
We go out in the world every day and make decisions about what is safe or not and what is appropriate or not. It is what psychologist Joseph LeDoux has suggested is an unconscious "danger detector" that determines whether or not something or someone is safe before we can even begin to consciously make a determination. When the object, animal, or person is assessed to be dangerous, a fear response, which has been called "fight or flight," occurs. On a conscious level we may correct a mistake in this "danger detector" when we notice it, but often, of course, we simply begin to generate reasons for why it was accurate in the first place. We are generally convinced that our decisions are "rational," but in reality, most human decisions are made emotionally, and then we collect or generate the facts to justify these decisions. When we see something or someone who "feels" dangerous, we have already launched into action internally before we have even started "thinking."
```

Original summary:
"We perform a(n) (A)___ assessment of danger before conscious thinking, making emotion-based decisions first and then generating reasons to (B)___ them."
Original answer: ① automatic / support

## Questions

### Q1: 요약문 (A)(B) — HARDER vocabulary pair (2점)
- **발문**: `다음은 윗글을 요약한 것이다. 빈칸 (A), (B)에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Summary**: *Our brains make (A)_____ judgments about danger before rational thought kicks in, and we then tend to (B)_____ those judgments with consciously generated reasoning.*
- **Correct**: (A) preconscious / (B) rationalize (= ③)
- **Choice table**:
  ```
       (A)               (B)
  ①  deliberate         challenge
  ②  preconscious       suppress
  ③  preconscious       rationalize    ← CORRECT
  ④  systematic         validate
  ⑤  instinctive        ignore
  ```
- **Why hard**: (A) "instinctive" is tempting (fight-or-flight = instinct). But "preconscious" more precisely captures "before we can consciously determine." (B) "validate" with (A)"systematic" is wrong — our decisions aren't systematic. (B) "suppress" means we hide the reasoning — but actually we GENERATE reasoning to support, not suppress.

### Q2: 빈칸추론 (2점)
Add a blank: Replace "generate reasons for why it was accurate" with `\rule{4.5cm}{0.4pt}`:
"we simply begin to _____ the danger detector's initial response"
- **발문**: `다음 빈칸에 들어갈 말로 가장 적절한 것은?`
- **Correct**: ④ justify
- **Choices**:
  - ① challenge
  - ② ignore
  - ③ override
  - ④ justify  ← CORRECT (= generate reasons why it was accurate = justify it)
  - ⑤ abandon
- **Why hard**: ① "challenge" is the exact opposite (passage says we DON'T challenge it, we confirm it). ③ "override" is what conscious reasoning *could* do, but the passage says we usually don't.
